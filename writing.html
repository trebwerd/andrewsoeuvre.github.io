Preface.

Writing can feel a lot like casting a fishing net out at sea. A sea filled with thoughts and dreams. I never quite know what I am going to catch until I pull my net aboard, sometimes it is terribly heavy, other times weightless. Seaweed? Fish? Crabs? I treat all as equals, for each can lead to an exciting adventure, or possibly a flavorsome sushi roll.

Naturally my net is always tangled, and so is everything in it, playfully intertwined. It is a mixture of both sporadic gyrations and utter stillness. The fun part is untangling my lucky catch and making sense of it all, while being grateful the sea has once again spoiled me with her riches.

What does the seaweed mean to the fish, and why have the crabs found their way to me? It need not matter the vessel that brings these gifts ashore, so long as I, the captain, am sure to sing shanties long into the evening as we set off for home.

Remain steadfast and cast the widest nets, scraping the floors of all seven seas, for you may leave with more of yourself than you came with.

Poems.

An amphibian sunrise

water the cold stars with tears

kick till the bloody creek clears

hold tight to the winding wave

all waking tadpoles are brave

Constellation no. 11

you fade into the ash

i'll scrape the stars for every lie

i know it's mad and brash

yet I can't help but search the sky

Abandoning ship

villages wander through the gold valley

wolves pillage all the northwestern galleys

do not squander this feeling please ponder

for even cold snowflakes tend to wander

Linden wood

a gentle hum or a sharp ring?

many hearts mended everything.

circles may clash as glass mocks cash,

inhale the ash, the chrome, the flash.

bursting suns fly behind the eyes,

trying to drown sorrows with highs.

years are shed as the lights flow past,

peers have bled whilst the knights held fast.

The animals

how does the phoenix find its way?

where is the lamb kneeling to pray?

when does the dog share all its bones?

why is it the pig casting stones?

what island is the parrot from?

who is the monkey calling scum?

the bear knows all but he won't say,

though the fish will tell without pay.

Untitled 7

sit within,

with fiery kin.

the fire, the flame.

the ember is to blame.

Black willow

why stir rocks in a pot

for the bear who was caught

under the willow that night

by fickle vines out of spite
